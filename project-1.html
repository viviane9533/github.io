DIVOC

During the 2021 Rose Hackathon, My team and I created a Covid-19 Sypmtom Trackers that gamifies the tracking experience by marking milestones and giving users awards.

Devpost Link

Company - Rose Hack 2021

Role - Product Designer and Frontend Developer

Focus - Login Page and User Interface

Award - Best UI/UX Hack 2021

Rose Hack 2021 was my first ever Hackathon and was hosted online due to COVID-19. I had the opportunity to create a group with some amazing people and design a project targeted for College students. We decided to go with a application used to track COVID-19 symptoms, to be used by schools all over. With it being my first hackathon, I learned to collaborate with others and brainstorm ideas together as a team about how the app was to run and look like. Here's a video about our application!

Problem

Many Schools as well as Companies use COVID-19 Symptom Trackers to track employees or students symptom to help decrease the spread of the COVID-19 Virus. As a result, they turn to generic Symptom Trackers and require their employees or students to use it daily. We conducted research with a survey to the Rose Hack participants that asks survey trackers if they have ever used the COVID-19 Symptom Tracker provided by their employer or school. Based on the results, we concluded that only 10% of the participants actually used it and this was because of its lack of user friendly features and loses the interest of the users.

Inspiration

While some university's are providing COVID-19 symptom trackers for their faculty, staff, and students, very few individuals use such tools. To motivate individuals to use such trackers, our app, Divoc gamifies the symptom tracking experience by marking milestones and giving users awards. By being more aware of both one's mental and physical health, Divoc hopes to do its part to reduce the spread of the coronavirus.
